# Oncology Biotech Bellwethers — Power Map and Watchlist Design (16 Jul 2026)

Research asset for the competitive-intelligence watchlist. Question: who are the true agenda-setters of oncology biotech — the OpenAI/Nvidia equivalents whose moves the industry copies — and what does that imply for a roster re-read twice weekly?

## Verdict: the analogy mostly FAILS — but not for the reason you'd expect

**No company in oncology plays the OpenAI role.** In AI, a lab ships a capability and rivals copy the *approach* within months. In oncology, the thing that gets copied is never a company's strategy — it is a **single clinical readout**, and the copying is capital reallocation, not imitation of method. Pharma is a **fast follower of data**, and it follows data emitted by organizations it otherwise ignores. The 2024–26 PD-1×VEGF stampede is the proof: a mid-cap Chinese biotech (Akeso) posted one Phase 3 PFS curve, and within ~9 months Merck, BMS/BioNTech and Pfizer had committed >$10B chasing the *mechanism* — while Akeso itself set no agenda before or since.

**Two entities do behave like genuine agenda-setters, and neither is a biotech.** The **FDA** changes industry behavior by fiat on a timescale of months-to-years (Project Optimus moved formal dose-optimization from <20% to a majority of early-phase oncology protocols in three years; the agency simply *refused* Summit's PFS-only filing and killed it). And the **Keytruda 2028 patent cliff** — a *deadline*, not an organization — is the single largest gravitational force in the sector, explaining Merck's ~$40B of 2026 buying better than any competitive-imitation story.

**Consequence for the watchlist: tracking companies is the wrong primitive.** The unit of agenda-setting is the **asset-modality-catalyst**, and companies are merely the addresses where catalysts are emitted. The honest design is a **standing roster of ~24 entities** (which exists only to catch the emissions) wrapped around a **rolling catalyst queue** that is the actual product. See §3.

## The power map

### Tier 0 — The thing that actually sets the agenda: the readout

The causal chain runs **data → capital**, never company → company. Two 2026 cases, both verifiable at primary sources:

**Revolution Medicines / daraxonrasib.** On 13 Apr 2026 RevMed reported topline Phase 3 RASolute 302 results in previously-treated metastatic pancreatic cancer: median OS **13.2 vs 6.7 months, HR 0.40, p<0.0001** (n=500), presented at the **ASCO 2026 late-breaking Plenary** and published in NEJM ([RevMed IR](https://ir.revmed.com/news-releases/news-release-details/daraxonrasib-demonstrates-unprecedented-overall-survival-benefit/), [ASCO plenary release](https://ir.revmed.com/news-releases/news-release-details/revolution-medicines-announces-asco-plenary-presentation/)). *Then* came the reported ~$28–32B Merck bid. The readout repriced the company; Merck reacted.

**Akeso / ivonescimab.** Akeso's Tetrabody-engineered PD-1/VEGF bispecific ([Akeso product page](https://www.akesobio.com/en/rd-and-science/products-center/ivonescimab/)) posted a China Phase 3 PFS win over pembrolizumab (HARMONi-2, WCLC 2024). The pile-on was immediate and *pre-proof*:

| Merck ← LaNova (PD-1×VEGF) | Nov 2024 (next day) | $588M upfront | [Fierce](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/pfizer-pays-3sbio-125b-pd-1xvegf-bispecific-joining-biontech-merck-and-summit-red-hot-race) |

Every one of those was signed **before** any PD-1×VEGF asset had a statistically significant OS win. That is genuinely OpenAI-analogy-like *velocity* — but note what was copied: a **target pair**, not a company's playbook. And AbbVie later joined the same table ([ApexOnco](https://www.oncologypipeline.com/apexonco/abbvie-next-vegf-bispecific-table)).

**The counter-case that proves pharma is not in charge.** Summit's global HARMONi trial (EGFR-mutant NSCLC post-TKI) returned OS 16.8 vs 14.0 months, **HR 0.79, p=0.057 against a 0.0448 bar** — a miss ([Fierce](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/summit-global-pd-1vegf-data-challenge-regional-consistency-claims-new-promising-survival)). FDA told Summit a submission could not be made without a statistically significant OS result. A ~$5B-headline asset with three Big Pharma imitators was stopped by one regulator's endpoint standard. **Then** at ASCO 2026, HARMONi-6 delivered: ivonescimab + chemo beat PD-1 + chemo on OS in first-line squamous NSCLC, **HR 0.66**, median OS 27.9 vs 23.7 months, Plenary Session ([Akeso/PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/harmoni-6-demonstrates-significant-overall-survival-benefit-hr0-66-ivonescimab-plus-chemotherapy-superior-to-pd-1-plus-chemotherapy-in-first-line-sq-nsclc-landmark-results-to-be-presented-at-asco-2026-plenary-session-302786433.html), [ASCO Post](https://ascopost.com/news/june-2026/overall-survival-benefit-with-ivonescimab-plus-chemotherapy-in-advanced-squamous-nsclc/)). ⚠ **Contested**: HARMONi-6 is a China-only trial; regional-generalizability is the live analyst dispute and the reason this is not yet a settled US standard of care.

**Corrections to the seeded brief:**
- **GSK/IDRx ($1.15B) is not 2026 context** — it was a JPM25 deal that **closed 21 Feb 2025** ([GSK](https://www.gsk.com/en-gb/media/press-releases/gsk-completes-acquisition-of-idrx-inc/)). $1B upfront + $150M regulatory milestone.
- **Novartis/Synnovation is not a $2B acquisition of the company** — it's a $2B-upfront carve-out of a subsidiary (Pikavation) holding the PI3Kα program. Synnovation continues to operate independently. (The seed's "$2B upfront" figure is nonetheless correct.)

**Who actually reprices?** Two different answers, and conflating them is the mistake:

- **Lilly sets the price of capacity.** Lilly committed **>$25B across ~10 deals in H1 2026** — more than half of the $46.4B spent by the top 12 pharmas combined ([BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/business/lillys-25b-m-a-spree-captures-half-of-pharmas-2026-capacity), [Endpoints](https://endpoints.news/eli-lilly-dominates-first-half-biotech-acquisitions-with-no-sign-of-slowing/)). But — critically — **most of it is not oncology** (Centessa/sleep $6.3B, Kelonia/cell therapy up to $7B, three vaccine companies ~$3.8B). Lilly is GLP-1 cash flooding *adjacent* therapeutic areas, which raises the clearing price every oncology buyer pays without Lilly setting any oncology agenda.
- **Merck is the most forced buyer in the industry.** Keytruda's US composition-of-matter patent expires **2028**, putting **>$25B/yr** at risk — the largest single LOE in oncology history. Merck's Terns, LaNova, Kelun and (reported) RevMed moves are all clock-driven. Merck's partial hedge is Keytruda Qlex, the subcutaneous formulation approved Sep 2025 with a fresh formulation/device patent estate ([BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/fda/as-exclusivity-loss-looms-merck-wins-subcutaneous-approval-for-keytruda)).

**This is the key structural finding: Merck is not copying anyone. It is running from a calendar.** The rest of oncology M&A is substantially the wake of that, plus Lilly's unrelated cash.

### Tier 2 — Platform / technology owners

**Daiichi Sankyo is the closest thing oncology has to Nvidia** — a picks-and-shovels position everyone else must either license or clone. Its **DXd** linker-payload chemistry, refined ~15 years, is the backbone of Enhertu (HER2), Datroway (TROP2) and three more clinical ADCs ([BioPharma Dive](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/daiichi-sankyo-okuzawa-asco-adc-cancer-drug-plans/749782/)). The AstraZeneca alliance — **$1.35B upfront + up to $5.6B milestones**, 50/50 co-dev across HER2/TROP2/B7-H3 — produced the highest-revenue ADC in history (~$3.75B combined 2024 Enhertu sales) ([Fierce](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/daiichi-sankyo-navigates-complexity-success-enhertu-datroway-push-market-dominance)). Daiichi/AZ set *technical* direction (payload class, DAR, target selection) in a way no PD-1 player ever did.

**Akeso's Tetrabody** is the second real platform claim — Fc-engineered tetravalent bispecific architecture with cooperative binding ([peer-reviewed: Antibody Therapeutics, Oxford](https://academic.oup.com/abt/article/9/2/191/8654276); [design paper, iScience/ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2589004224029493)). Unlike most China platforms, it is documented in the literature, not just in press releases.

**Radiopharma** is the live platform land-grab — Novartis (Pluvicto/Lutathera) is the incumbent; Aktis is the challenger (below). **Bispecific ADCs** are the next payload frontier and where VC is concentrating.

### Tier 3 — China: sets the *price*, not the *direction*

This is the most-inflated area of the seeded brief. What survives verification:

- **~$136B** in Chinese outbound licensing in 2025, **~$60B in Q1 2026 alone (+73% YoY)**, now **~one-third of global out-licensing value**, up from ~a fifth in 2023–24 ([Reuters analysis via Yahoo](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/analysis-china-biotech-licensing-boom-092338804.html), [SCMP](https://www.scmp.com/business/china-business/article/3348295/china-biotech-deals-hit-record-innovative-drugs-draw-interest-multinationals), [Goldman Sachs](https://www.goldmansachs.com/insights/articles/china-is-increasing-its-share-of-global-drug-development)).
- **Upfronts rose 230%: $52M (2022) → $172M (2026 YTD)** — confirmed by Fierce, and the framing is the point: *"China is no longer the bargain basement"* ([Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/analyst-china-no-longer-bargain-basement-biotech-acquisitions)). **This is the single most watchlist-relevant China fact.**
- **Hengrui is the volume price-setter**: GSK **$500M upfront / up to $12.5B**, 12 programs, Jul 2025 ([GSK](https://www.gsk.com/en-gb/media/press-releases/gsk-and-hengrui-pharma-enter-agreements/)); BMS **$600M upfront / up to $15.2B**, 13 early-stage programs, May 2026 ([BioPharma Dive](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/bristol-myers-hengrui-china-biotech-drugs-deal/819937/)). ⚠ The seeded ">$50B cumulative deal value" is **UNVERIFIED** — I can only add ~$27.7B from these two confirmed alliances.
- **Kelun-Biotech**: sac-TMT (SKB264/MK-2870) is the anchor of a multi-target Merck ADC alliance and is **approved in China** for 3L+ TNBC ([Fierce](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/keluns-merck-partnered-adc-snags-first-nod-kick-trop2-fight-gilead)). ⚠ The "**$7B+**" Merck figure is **UNVERIFIED** — no primary source located. Separately, **Crescent Biopharma** licensed **SKB105** for **$80M upfront + up to $1.25B milestones** (SEC-filed) — so the seed's "~$1.25B" is milestones, not deal value.
- **DualityBio → Avenzo**: EGFR/HER3 bispecific ADC (AVZO-1418/DB-1418), **$50M upfront + ~$1.15B milestones** — and it is a **Jan 2025** deal, not 2026 ([Avenzo press release](https://avenzotx.com/press-releases/avenzo-therapeutics-and-dualitybio-announce-exclusive-global-license-for-potential-best-in-class-egfr-her3-antibody-drug-conjugate/)).

**⚠ FALSIFIED / UNSUPPORTED: "China accounts for ~90% of global ADC licensing activity."** Every instance of this claim traces to **visionlifesciences.com**, an SEO content farm explicitly out of scope as a citation of record. No primary or reputable-trade source corroborates it. The defensible adjacent claims are that **>50% of global clinical-stage ADC programs are China-run** and **~30% of novel candidates in global clinical development are China-origin** — and even these should be treated as **soft** (the >50% figure also surfaces mainly via aggregators; the ~30% is Goldman-supported). Treat the 90% number as **fiction until proven otherwise**.

Likewise **"Argo"** and **"Parabilis"** could not be verified as meaningful agenda-setters in any source; **UNVERIFIED**, do not seed them into the roster.

**The correct read on China:** Chinese biotechs are the **supply side and the clearing-price mechanism** for pre-clinical/early-clinical assets. They determine what a TROP2 ADC or a PD-1×VEGF *costs*. They do not determine that TROP2 or PD-1×VEGF *matter* — that was determined by Daiichi's DXd data and Akeso's Phase 3 curves respectively. Price-setter ≠ agenda-setter.

### Tier 4 — Regulators/payers: the only true agenda-setters

**FDA is the real bellwether, and it just consolidated power.** Richard Pazdur — 26-year veteran, founding director of the Oncology Center of Excellence — is now **director of CDER** ([FDA](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/longtime-fda-innovator-appointed-director-center-drug-evaluation-and-research), [Fierce](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/fda-names-longtime-oncology-chief-richard-pazdur-its-new-cder-director)). The oncology reviewer's worldview now governs all of drug evaluation. Evidence FDA changes behavior *fast*:

- **Project Optimus** moved formal dose-optimization plans in early-phase oncology protocols from **<20% (2021) to a majority (2024)** after the Aug 2024 final guidance ([FDA](https://www.fda.gov/about-fda/oncology-center-excellence/project-optimus); [JCO Oncology Advances](https://ascopubs.org/doi/10.1200/OA-25-00084)). No company achieves that kind of universal adoption.
- **Accelerated approval is tightening**: confirmatory trials must be underway as a condition of AA. And FDA's refusal of Summit's PFS-only filing (§Tier 0) is a clean demonstration that one endpoint standard can nullify a multibillion-dollar thesis overnight.
- **⚠ New and genuinely destabilizing: the Commissioner's National Priority Voucher (CNPV) pilot** compresses review from 10–12 months to **1–2 months** for products aligned to five national priorities (affordability, domestic manufacturing, unmet need) ([FDA program page](https://www.fda.gov/industry/commissioners-national-priority-voucher-cnpv-pilot-program)). At least **seven approvals** have been granted under it ([FDA](https://www.fda.gov/news-events/press-announcements/fda-grants-seventh-approval-under-national-priority-voucher-pilot-program)), and a public hearing ran Mar 2026 ([Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/03/23/2026-05573/commissioners-national-priority-voucher-cnpv-pilot-program-public-hearing-request-for-comments)). **Contested inside FDA itself** — Pazdur has publicly questioned the safety and legality of expedited pathways including CNPV ([BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/fda/pazdur-questions-legality-safety-of-fdas-expedited-drug-approvals-programs)). A CDER director at odds with the Commissioner's flagship program is a live institutional fracture and a top watch item.

**CMS/IRA — the "pill penalty" is a real capital-allocation input, but the headline number is lobbying.** Small molecules face negotiation at **9 years** post-launch vs **13 for biologics**; the EPIC Act would equalize at 13, the ORPHAN Cures Act would widen the rare-disease carve-out ([MassBio](https://www.massbio.org/news/recent-news/fixing-the-ira-the-epic-act-mini-act-and-orphan-cures-act/), [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/policy/congress-re-introduces-epic-act-to-remove-iras-pill-penalty)). ⚠ The widely-repeated **"small-molecule investment has plummeted 70%"** originates from industry advocacy organs (Bio.News/ASBM), not an independent study — **treat as contested lobbying claim**. Reform faces "stiff budgetary headwinds" ([BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/policy/pushback-against-iras-controversial-pill-penalty-faces-stiff-budgetary-headwinds)). Note the tension with Tier 0: RevMed's daraxonrasib is an **oral small molecule** — exactly the class the pill penalty disadvantages — and Merck is reportedly bidding ~$30B for it anyway. The pill penalty is a real thumb on the scale, not a veto.

**EMA and NMPA are followers, with one asterisk.** NMPA approves China-origin assets first (ivonescimab, sac-TMT) which creates the *data* that moves Western capital — but NMPA sets no global standard. EMA is a genuine laggard in agenda-setting terms.

### Tier 5 — Capital allocators: a lagging indicator, not a leading one

The capital tier is where the OpenAI analogy most clearly collapses: VCs in oncology **follow validated modalities**, they do not pick them.

- **Aktis Oncology**: 2026's first biotech IPO — **$318M** at $18/sh, ~$1B valuation, upsized, closed up 27% ([BioPharma Dive](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/aktis-biotech-ipo-radiopharmaceutical-cancer/809066/), [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/business/radiopharma-focused-aktis-squeezes-in-ipo-to-end-biotech-drought)). **Lilly anchored with ~$100M (≈1/3 of the book)** — the seeded claim is **CONFIRMED**, and it builds on a May 2024 collaboration ($60M upfront + equity, up to $1.1B milestones). Strategic-investor-as-IPO-anchor is a real signal of where a platform is being validated.
- **Sidewinder Therapeutics**: **$137M Series B**, Apr 2026, co-led Frazier + **Novartis Venture Fund**, with OrbiMed, Goldman Sachs Alternatives, DCVC Bio, Astellas Venture — precision **bispecific ADCs**; IND for SWT012 targeted end-2026 ([Sidewinder/Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260408583837/en/Sidewinder-Therapeutics-Announces-$137-Million-Series-B-Financing-to-Advance-Precision-Bispecific-ADCs-into-Clinical-Development-for-Cancer)). **CONFIRMED.**
- **Ona Therapeutics**: **$86.6M Series B**, 4 Jun 2026, co-led Columbus Venture Partners + Mérieux; first-in-class ADCs ONA-255 (breast), ONA-389 (CRC) ([GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/06/04/3306533/0/en/ona-therapeutics-raises-86-6-million-series-b-to-advance-first-in-class-adcs-into-clinical-development.html)). **CONFIRMED.**
- **Isomorphic Labs**: **$2.1B Series B** (12 May 2026, led by Thrive; Alphabet, GV, MGX, Temasek, CapitalG, UK Sovereign AI Fund) — one of the largest AI-drug-discovery raises ever ([Fierce](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/alphabets-ai-biotech-isomorphic-labs-bags-21b-series-b-fuel-next-gen-drug-design-model), [Isomorphic](https://www.isomorphiclabs.com/articles/isomorphic-labs-announces-series-b-investment-round)). ⚠ But **first-in-human is a 2026 target that has already slipped from end-2025**, and sources conflict on whether trials have actually dosed anyone — the "human trials launched" claims come only from low-trust aggregators. **Zero clinical data = zero agenda-setting power in oncology.** Enormous capital, no evidence.

Note the pattern across all four: **three of four are ADC/radioligand plays validated by Daiichi and Novartis years earlier.** Capital is confirming Tier 2, not leading it.

### Tier 6 — Frontier biotechs: a shrinking, self-consuming tier

The 2026 lesson is that **frontier biotechs get acquired before they can set an agenda**. Of the seeded list, **Arcellx** (Gilead, Apr) and **Nuvalent** (GSK, Jul) are already gone — inside the six months the brief covers. Revolution Medicines is reportedly next. Any watchlist built on this tier has a half-life measured in months, which is itself an argument for tracking assets rather than tickers: *the asset survives the acquisition; the entity does not.*

## Recommended watchlist

### The count: ~24 standing entities, and the roster is the wrong product

Arguing from structure, not from a round number:

1. **Agenda-setting emissions are episodic and calendared.** ASCO, ESMO, AACR, WCLC, ASH, plus JPMorgan in January. Both 2026 repricing events in this report (daraxonrasib, HARMONi-6) landed at a **single ASCO plenary**. A twice-weekly cadence will find *nothing* for weeks and then face a firehose in one 72-hour window. A fixed-size company roster is systematically mis-shaped for this.
2. **The primitive should be the asset/modality/catalyst.** Track **daraxonrasib**, not Revolution Medicines — because when Merck closes, the entity vanishes and the asset (and the RAS(ON) thesis) keeps setting the agenda inside Merck. Track **PD-1×VEGF**, not Akeso — because the mechanism now spans Akeso, Summit, BMS, BioNTech, Pfizer, 3SBio, Merck, LaNova and AbbVie, and no single ticker covers it.
3. **Entities remain necessary as subscription addresses.** You cannot RSS-feed a modality. The roster's only job is to be the set of IR pages, SEC filers and regulator feeds where catalysts surface.
4. **~24 is what the structure yields**: 6 acquirers (the only ones with the balance sheet to reprice), 5 China supply-side price-setters, 3 platform owners, 3 regulators, 4–5 frontier assets, ~2 rotating slots. Below ~20 you lose a tier; above ~30 you are adding entities that have never once moved the market.

**Recommended shape: ~24 standing entities + a rolling catalyst queue of 10–15 named assets/readouts, re-cut monthly against the conference calendar.** The queue is the deliverable. The roster is plumbing.
